# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indie founders and solopreneurs — often neurodivergent or ND-adjacent — doing serious market research before building or positioning a product. They're not doing this recreationally. They're in a research moment that feels high-stakes: mapping customer pain, competitive landscape, and market structure so they can make a real decision. They need depth, not speed. They work at irregular hours in irregular rhythms. They distrust tools that feel like they're being sold to.

## Product Purpose

Category Scout runs six research phases in parallel using Exa deep search, then surfaces a structured strategy layer and intelligence brief. The research → strategy → agent brief pipeline gives founders evidence-backed positioning and a low-contact distribution plan — built around how they actually work, not how productivity culture says they should. Success looks like: a founder walks away knowing exactly what to do first, with a brief they can hand to an AI agent to execute.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, structured, quiet, sharp, honest. Tool-first — the work speaks, not the chrome. No decoration without purpose. Confidence through clarity, not spectacle.

## Anti-references

- SaaS dashboard aesthetic: gradients, glassmorphism, hero metrics, glowing dark mode (Notion, Linear, Vercel's marketing)
- AI tool aesthetic: neural-net animations, "powered by AI" visual language, Cursor/Perplexity energy
- Growth hacker tools: bright CTAs, conversion pressure baked into UI, HubSpot/Semrush urgency
- Consultant report PDFs: heavy tables, corporate grey, McKinsey deck structure without warmth

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the work.** No chrome for its own sake. Every visual element should either carry information or get out of the way.
2. **Calm is a feature.** ND users need an interface that doesn't spike demand. No urgency cues, no "you should be doing more" signals, no loading anxiety.
3. **Structure over decoration.** Typography, spacing, and proportion do the visual work. Color is used sparingly — as signal, not wallpaper.
4. **Evidence earns hierarchy.** Things that hold more research weight look more substantial. Not by shouting — by density and presence.
5. **Honest feedback over polish theater.** Empty states tell you what's missing, not what you could be doing if you upgraded.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG standard currently enforced. Build thoughtfully: ND-aware pacing (no time pressure, no animated urgency), low cognitive load per screen, progressive disclosure for complexity. Reduced motion respected via system preference.
