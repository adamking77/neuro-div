# V1

This framework is sharp until the exact step that matters — then it vanishes.

Step 1: write the messy paragraph. Then the spine supposedly "emerges through honest reflection." That's not a method. That's the hard part, deleted and relabeled as a feeling.

So you dump the paragraph and you're left staring at it, waiting to feel your way to a single integrating question out of nothing. I didn't feel anything. I stopped. And then I did the thing this essay should've predicted: I assumed I was the problem.

I wasn't. The framework quietly assumes a brain that feels first and finds the words after. A lot of neurocomplex minds run the opposite direction — we need a candidate built and put in front of us before the gut can do its job. Then it's instant: no, no, closer, that one. 

We're not bad at this. We're elite at recognition and useless at generating from a void — and "reflect until it emerges" is a void.

Which means the people this essay is most for are the ones most likely to freeze at step one. That's not a small gap. That's the engine missing from the car.

If you froze here too — you didn't fail it. It's incomplete. The middle's not there.
