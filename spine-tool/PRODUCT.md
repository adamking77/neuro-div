# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Neurodivergent people (ADHD, autism, dyslexia, and related profiles) who are structure-first thinkers. They freeze on open-ended reflection prompts and need built objects to react to before feeling can do its job. They read carefully, distrust vague promises, and are sensitive to cognitive overload.

## Product Purpose

The Spine-Finder Architecture page explains a process scaffold that turns raw self-analysis into concrete orientation candidates. It exists to fill the gap in Lindsey Mackereth's existing framework: "reflect until it emerges" is not a method for structure-first minds. The page must communicate the architecture clearly without overwhelming the reader — progressive reveal over wall-of-content.

## Brand Personality

Calm, instrument-like, precise. Editorial restraint. The product earns trust by showing receipts, not performing warmth.

## Anti-references

- Glossy SaaS landing pages with gradients and "AI-powered" hero stats
- Therapy or wellness apps with soft pastels and inspirational quotes
- Productivity tools that use rocket emoji and aggressive CTAs
- Dense academic documents with no visual breathing room

## Design Principles

1. **Show the object before asking for feeling.** The reader should always have something concrete to evaluate before being asked to judge or scroll forward.
2. **Breathing room is signal.** Generous whitespace communicates that this tool respects cognitive load. Crowding is an anti-pattern.
3. **Motion teaches, not decorates.** Animations reveal structure — they show how pieces relate, not just that they exist.
4. **Non-final is a valid state.** The design should communicate that partial progress is real progress. No forced conclusions.
5. **Receipts visible.** Every claim traces to evidence. The UI should model this in how it reveals information progressively.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Neurodivergent-aware: no sensory overload, no rapid flashing, no auto-playing movement. Focus rings visible. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable.
